01 / Plan

Tu plan de batalla — in order.

1

File an FCC + BBB complaint.

Five minutes online. AT&T's Office of the President answers these inside 48 hours — way more authority than retention to fix billing, swap your plan, or hand out credits.

Highest leverage
2

Then call Loyalty.

Dial 611 or 800-331-0500. Say "cancel service" to get routed. Be specific: "Rep promised unlimited high-speed. I'm being throttled at 5GB. Fix it or release me."

Backup
3

Pull the store visit notes.

AT&T logs every interaction. If the in-store rep promised what the plan can't deliver, that's documentable. Ask the agent to read them aloud.

Evidence
4

Ask for a supervisor.

If the first agent dead-ends you, escalate. Don't argue with someone who can't actually fix it. Move up until you find someone who can.

Escalate
The trade-in credit is tied to a 36-month installment. They will resist letting you walk. That is your leverage — not your weakness. — Field note, Desk 04
02 / Ask

What you're actually asking for.

Three outcomes. Pick what fits.

Don't walk into the call without knowing what "win" looks like. Pick one. State it clearly. Then hold the line.

  • Plan upgrade at current price. Move to Unlimited Premium (no 5GB throttle) — same monthly cost you were quoted. The cleanest fix.
  • Bill credits for the difference. If they won't change the plan, credit the gap monthly until your installment ends. Math it out — get the dollar amount in writing.
  • Release from the device installment. The nuclear option. If they refuse to honor the sale, let you walk away from the iPhone 16 financing without penalty. Leverage only — most people don't actually want this.
